About Us

Experience is the teacher in the InspirEd classroom

Vision
to inspire today's students to become tomorrow's servant leaders.
Mission
to advance the science and practice of experiential learning in information technology.

InspirEd Lab is a community of students, educators, designers, researchers and developers working on experiments ideated to push into new spaces the development of experiential learning in information technology. The Lab provides an open, collaborative environment for interdisciplinary exploration, where students and professionals learn together as a team.

Courage
to do the right thing and work on tough problems.
Focus
on the goals of the team.
Commitment
to personally achieving the goals of the team.
Respect
each other to be capable, independent people.
Openness
about all the work and the challenges of performing it.

We rise up to the challenge embrace change and are energized by working on difficult problems worth solving; we believe in the process as much as the outcome. We are probably best known for our innovative Agile Classroom approach and our set of open-source adaptable tools for implementing it. We also develop prototypes of tools for reporting, data management, research, and development, often in connection with Northern Illinois University courses.

We believe in challenges

"The brick walls are there for a reason. The brick walls are not there to keep us out. The brick walls are there to give us a chance to show how badly we want something." - Randy Pausch

InspirEd Lab is an immersive learning experience where NIU students, faculty, and professionals collaborate at the junction of storytelling, design, and information technology. Taking on challenging projects, we research innovative approaches and develop solutions employing cutting-edge tools and techniques â€” validating learning along every step of the exploratory path.

Our approach combines user experience research, design thinking, lean and agile principles, iterative development, active learning, storytelling and a healthy dose of experimentation.

Our most successful students are driven and motivated; they possess the curiosity and determination to drive and sustain a project from start to finish. They are comfortable with ambiguity and have a strong desire to identify lines of questioning and paths to find the answers.
